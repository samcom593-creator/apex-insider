The Illinois license in 30 days

The Illinois Department of Insurance issues a Life & Health line that runs about 4-6 weeks from study to test to walking-around-with-your-license. Cost: typically $150-$250 all in (fingerprints + exam + license issuance). Anyone telling you you need to pay $5,000 to "join" their team is lying — you do not.

Why Illinois is a good place to start

  • Reciprocity: Illinois reciprocates with most other states; adding states later is paperwork-only.
  • Carrier appetite: Most major carriers (Mutual of Omaha, AIG, Americo, AHL, Royal Neighbors) write in Illinois aggressively.
  • Final-expense demand: Senior-market final-expense is strong in Illinois — easiest entry-level product.

The 30-day path

  1. Week 1: Buy a study course. ExamFX, Kaplan, or A.D. Banker. 1-2 hours/day.
  2. Week 2: Take the exam. Pass with 70-85%. Schedule your fingerprint appointment.
  3. Week 3: Apply for the license. Most Illinois agents get their number within 5-7 business days.
  4. Week 4: Contract with carriers through your FMO. Make your first 20 dials. Close your first deal.

What NOT to do

  • Pay an FMO $200+ upfront before you've sold a single policy.
  • Sign a captive contract that says you can't keep your book if you leave.
  • Buy leads in your first 30 days. Get the license + your team first.
  • Pick a state you don't live in as your first license. Pick Illinois, then expand.
